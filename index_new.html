About Me

I investigate the intelligence encoded inside a deep neural network.

I am a lifelong proud gator and a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at Boston University, advised by Prof. Kayhan Batmanghelich at Batman Lab and Dr. Clare B. Poynton from Boston University Medical Campus.

My research focuses on robustness and generalization in deep learning, with a particular emphasis on leveraging vision-language representations to understand, explain, and audit pre-trained neural networks. My work has been published at top peer-reviewed venues including ACL, NAACL, ICML, MICCAI, AMIA, JAMIA, and RAD:AI. I believe that understanding a model’s behavior is essential to mitigating bias and building trust in AI systems.

In the summers of 2024 and 2025, I interned as an Applied Scientist at Amazon (AWS SAAR and AWS Optimus) in New York City and Pasadena. Before moving to Boston, I was a Ph.D. student at the University of Pittsburgh and cross-registered at CMU. Prior to my PhD journey, I obtained my Master's in CS from the University of Florida.

Research Focus

My core research interest lies in representation learning across computer vision and medical imaging, with a particular focus on interpretability and explainable AI. I aim to answer questions such as:

  • Can we decipher failure modes of deep models using vision-language representations? (LADDER, ACL 2025)
  • Can we learn robust representations in presence of multiple biases? (NeurIPS TRL 2024)
  • Can we learn robust VL representations with limited data efficiently? (Mammo-CLIP, MICCAI 2024)
  • Can we extract interpretable models from blackbox representations? (MoIE, ICML 2023)
