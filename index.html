Research

My research interests lie in computer vision, deep learning, causal inference and their medical applications focusing on basic to advanced architectures of supervised and unsupervised deep neural networks to improve their generalization, and interpretability. I am particularly interested in developing explainable AI (X-AI) algorithms to (1) understand the rich internal structure of blackbox deep neural network (2) develop the reasoning methodology in terms of high level interpretable concepts in an image (3) bridge the gap between post-hoc explainability and interpretable by design methods.

DR-VIDAL-Doubly Robust Variational Information-theoretic Deep Adversarial Learning for Counterfactual Prediction and Treatment Effect Estimation
Shantanu Ghosh, Zheng Feng , Jiang Bian , Kevin Butler , Mattia Prosperi
AMIA Annual Symposium, 2022. \Oral, to appear
[arXiv] [code] [supp]

We develop a novel deep learning framework which generates the counterfactual outcomes based on a treatment using GAN and information theoretic regularization. Next, we utilize the counterfactual outcomes to estimate the ITE using a novel deep learning network with doubly robust optimization for fast convergence.

Anatomy-Guided Weakly-Supervised Abnormality Localization in Chest X-rays
Ke Yu, Shantanu Ghosh, Zhexiong Li, Christopher Deible, Kayhan Batmanghelich
Proceedings of the International Conference on Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention (MICCAI), 2022
[pdf] [code]

We develop a deep learning network, AnatomyGuided chest X-ray Network (AGXNet), which leverage the radiology reports to create an anatomy-guided attention module using the weak labels of the anatomy mentions in the report to localize the disease and anatomical abnormality.
